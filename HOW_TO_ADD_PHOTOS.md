## Quick Guide

Yes! You can add your photos directly as files instead of URLs. Here's how:

## Step 1: Prepare Your Photos

1. **Get your photos** from your website or computer
2. **Rename them** to match the expected file names (see list below)
3. **Supported formats**: `.jpg`, `.jpeg`, `.png`, `.webp`

## Step 2: Add Photos to the Directory

### For Travel Blog Photos:
1. Open the folder: `assets/img/travel/`
2. Copy your photos into this folder
3. Make sure the file names match the list below

### For News Photos (optional):
1. Open the folder: `assets/img/news/`
2. Copy your news photos here

## Step 3: File Names Required

- Add more as needed!

## Step 4: Verify

1. Refresh your travel blog page
2. Check that photos appear correctly
3. Make sure each photo matches the correct location

## Tips

- **File size**: Keep images under 2MB for faster loading
- **Naming**: Use lowercase, hyphens for spaces (e.g., `hong-kong.jpg`)
- **Matching**: Double-check that each photo matches the correct location!

## Need Help?

If you have photos with different names, you can either:
1. Rename them to match the list above, OR
2. Tell me the file names and I can update the code to match them

## Example

If you have a photo called `MyPhuketTrip2025.png`:
1. Rename it to `phuket.jpg` (or keep as `.png`)
2. Place it in `assets/img/travel/phuket.jpg`
3. The website will automatically use it!

---

**That's it!** Once you add the photos to the `assets/img/travel/` folder, they'll automatically appear on your travel blog page.
